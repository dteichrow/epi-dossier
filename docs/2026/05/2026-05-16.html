    4. Ebola outbreak in Congo’s Ituri province: 246 suspected cases, 65 dead as region on alert
      Mathrubhumi English2026-05-16T04:58+00:00Outbreaks and emerging infections

      Africa’s top public health agency, the Africa Centres for Disease Control and Prevention, has confirmed a new Ebola outbreak in eastern Democratic Republic of the Congo’s Ituri province, with 246 suspected cases and 65 deaths reported so far, marking the country’s 17th outbreak since the virus was first detected in 1976. Health officials said only four deaths have been laboratory-confirmed so far, but surveillance teams moved to classify the outbreak after a sharp rise in suspected infections across multiple health zones in Ituri, including Mongwalu, Rwampara, and the provincial capital Bunia. Early indications suggest a variant linked to the Ebola Zaire lineage, which was responsible for several major outbreaks in the past, including the 2018–2020 epidemic that killed over 1,000 people.

      PubMed Infectious Disease Search2026-05-12T00:00Major epidemiology studies Official

      Timely protection with the autumn dose for those aged ≥75 y and the high-risk population aged 18-74 was estimated to avert 1.0 million cases, 15,642 hospitalization, 3,897 deaths, 82,977 post-COVID condition cases, £179 million in direct medical costs, and £195 million in productivity losses.     2. Modeling the potential public health and economic impact and cost-effectiveness of vaccination strategies using an adapted COVID-19 vaccine in Guatemala.
      PubMed Infectious Disease Search2026-05-15T00:00Major epidemiology studies Official

      Compared to no vaccination, the vaccination of older adults aged ≥65 years and the high-risk population was estimated to prevent 12,933 symptomatic cases, 12,811 outpatient cases, 122 hospitalizations, 353 lost quality-adjusted life-years (QALYs) gained, and four deaths, translating to total direct and societal cost savings of US$9,424,605 and US$10,397,107 respectively. Implementing an adapted COVID-19 vaccine strategy for high-risk and older adults in Guatemala is projected to improve health outcomes and, under base-case assumptions, be cost-saving from both payer and societal perspectives.
